Algebra

What it is: Algebra is the branch of mathematics dealing with symbols and the rules for manipulating them.

Key Concepts:

  • Variables, constants, coefficients
  • Linear equations: ax + b = 0 → Solve for x
  • Quadratic equations: ax² + bx + c = 0 → Factorise, quadratic formula, completing the square
  • Inequalities: Solve & graph solutions on a number line
  • Functions: Mapping inputs to outputs

Example: Solve 2x + 3 = 7 → 2x = 4 → x = 2

Tips: Always simplify expressions first; check for common factors.

Geometry

What it is: Geometry is the study of shapes, sizes, and properties of space.

Key Concepts:

  • Triangles: Types (equilateral, isosceles, scalene), Pythagoras theorem, congruence, similarity
  • Circles: Radius, diameter, circumference, area
  • Polygons: Interior & exterior angles, perimeter & area formulas
  • Coordinate Geometry: Distance formula, midpoint, slope of a line

Example: Find the hypotenuse of a right triangle with sides 3cm & 4cm → 5cm

Tips: Always draw diagrams and label all sides/angles.

Calculus

What it is: Calculus studies rates of change (derivatives) and accumulation (integrals).

Key Concepts:

  • Derivative: slope of a function at a point, rate of change
  • Integration: area under curves, ∫x^n dx = x^(n+1)/(n+1) + C
  • Limits: behaviour as x → value, continuity
  • Applications: velocity, acceleration, optimisation problems

Example: d/dx of x² = 2x

Tips: Memorise standard derivatives & integrals; practice word problems.

Statistics

What it is: Statistics is the study of data: collection, analysis, interpretation, presentation.

Key Concepts:

  • Measures of Central Tendency: Mean, Median, Mode
  • Measures of Spread: Range, Variance, Standard Deviation
  • Probability: P(event) = favourable / total outcomes
  • Graphs: Bar charts, histograms, pie charts, scatter plots

Example: Mean of 2, 4, 6 = (2+4+6)/3 = 4

Tips: Always check calculations; label axes on graphs.

Trigonometry

What it is: Study of relationships between angles and sides of triangles.

Key Concepts:

  • Sine, Cosine, Tangent: sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse, cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse, tan θ = opposite/adjacent
  • Pythagorean Identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • Unit Circle, radians vs degrees
  • Solving right-angled triangles

Example: In a triangle, opposite = 3, hypotenuse = 5 → sin θ = 3/5

Tips: Memorise key ratios and unit circle values.
