Biology

Photosynthesis & Respiration

  • Photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
  • Respiration (Aerobic): C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + energy (ATP)
  • Respiration (Anaerobic in humans): C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₃H₆O₃ + energy

Human Anatomy

  • Circulatory: heart, arteries, veins, capillaries; double circulation
  • Respiratory: lungs, alveoli, gas exchange, breathing mechanics
  • Digestive: mouth → esophagus → stomach → small intestine → large intestine
  • Nervous: brain, spinal cord, peripheral nerves; reflex arc basics

Plant Biology

  • Photosynthesis (formula above), chloroplasts, light-dependent vs light-independent reactions
  • Transpiration: water movement through xylem → evaporation from leaves
  • Plant reproduction: flowers, pollination, fertilisation, seed formation

Ecosystems

  • Food chains & webs, energy flow, producers/consumers/decomposers
  • Biomes: tropical rainforest, desert, tundra, grassland
  • Conservation & human impact

Tips: Draw diagrams, practise Punnett squares, memorise key formulas, label cells/organs, and summarise each system in bullet points.

Chemistry

Key Areas: Atomic structure, periodic table, bonding, reactions, stoichiometry, acids & bases, energy changes.

Atomic Structure

  • Protons (+), Neutrons (neutral), Electrons (−)
  • Atomic number = # of protons; Mass number = protons + neutrons
  • Isotopes: same protons, different neutrons (e.g., Carbon-12, Carbon-14)
  • Electron configuration: 1s² 2s² 2p⁶ ...

Periodic Table

  • Groups = vertical columns, similar properties
  • Periods = horizontal rows, same number of electron shells
  • Trends: Atomic radius ↓ across period, ↑ down group; Reactivity varies
  • Metals: left & centre; Nonmetals: right

Chemical Bonding

  • Ionic: transfer of electrons; metal + non-metal → lattice; high melting point
  • Covalent: sharing electrons; non-metal + non-metal; can be polar/nonpolar
  • Metallic: delocalised electrons, metal lattice; conducts electricity

Chemical Reactions

  • Combustion: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O
  • Neutralisation: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
  • Displacement: Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu
  • Decomposition: 2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂
  • Redox reactions: combination of oxidation & reduction

Stoichiometry & Moles

  • Moles: n = mass / molar mass
  • Concentration (solution): c = n / V
  • Avogadro’s number: 6.022 × 10²³ particles/mol
  • Limiting reagent: reactant that runs out first determines product
  • Example: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O; 4 mol H₂ + 2 mol O₂ → 4 mol H₂O

Acids & Bases

  • pH = −log[H⁺]
  • Strong acids fully dissociate (HCl → H⁺ + Cl⁻)
  • Weak acids partially dissociate (CH₃COOH ⇌ H⁺ + CH₃COO⁻)
  • Neutralisation: Acid + Base → Salt + Water
  • Indicators: Litmus, phenolphthalein, methyl orange

Energy Changes

  • Exothermic: releases energy (ΔH < 0), e.g., combustion
  • Endothermic: absorbs energy (ΔH > 0), e.g., photosynthesis
  • ΔH = H_products − H_reactants
  • Activation energy: minimum energy required for reaction

Tips: Memorise key formulas, practice balancing reactions, know group trends, and write stepwise solutions for stoichiometry problems.

Physics

Key Areas: Motion, forces, energy, waves, electricity, magnetism, atomic physics.

Motion

  • Distance (s), displacement (x), speed (v), velocity (v), acceleration (a)
  • Key formulas:
    • v = u + at
    • s = ut + ½at²
    • v² = u² + 2as
    • a = Δv / t
  • Graphs: distance-time → speed; velocity-time → acceleration & area = displacement

Forces

  • Newton’s laws:
    • 1st: Object in motion stays in motion unless acted on
    • 2nd: F = ma
    • 3rd: Every action has equal & opposite reaction
  • Weight: W = mg
  • Work done: W = F × d (J)
  • Pressure: P = F / A (Pa)
  • Friction, tension, normal force concepts

Energy

  • Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv²
  • Potential Energy: PE = mgh
  • Gravitational potential energy (GPE) = mgh
  • Work: W = F × d; Power: P = W / t
  • Efficiency: Efficiency = (useful energy / total energy) × 100%
  • Energy conservation principle: total energy remains constant in a closed system

Waves

  • Wave speed: v = fλ
  • Types: Transverse (light), Longitudinal (sound)
  • Reflection, refraction, diffraction
  • Sound waves: frequency (f), wavelength (λ), speed (v)
  • Electromagnetic spectrum: radio → gamma; all transverse waves

Electricity

  • Current (I), Voltage (V), Resistance (R)
  • Ohm’s Law: V = IR
  • Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R
  • Series circuits: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + ...
  • Parallel circuits: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ...

Magnetism & Electromagnetism

  • Magnetic fields: field lines from N → S
  • Force on current-carrying wire: F = BIL sinθ
  • Electromagnets: current + coil + iron core → magnet
  • Motor effect & Fleming’s left-hand rule

Atomic Physics

  • Radioactivity: α, β, γ radiation
  • Half-life: N = N₀ × (½)^(t / t½)
  • Isotopes: same protons, different neutrons
  • Nuclear reactions: fission (splitting), fusion (joining)

Tips: Memorise formulas with units, practise calculations, label diagrams, and do graph interpretation exercises.
